Tutorial 06 — Antibiotic PK/PD¶

Prerequisites: Tutorials 01–05

Phage therapy is most effective when combined with antibiotics: phage eradicate the bulk of replicating bacteria, while antibiotics reach the compartments that phage cannot — dormant populations and deep tissues. This tutorial introduces antibiotic pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics through the with_antibiotic() builder API.

What you will learn

  1. Two-compartment antibiotic PK (k_elim, k12, k21, Vc)
  2. Hill-equation PD (emax, ec50, hill) and the plot_pk visualisation
  3. Bacteriostatic vs bactericidal effects
  4. Per-strain resistance — antibiotic emax/ec50 arrays
  5. Dormant-cell activity (emax_dormant, ec50_dormant)
  6. Clinical dosing regimens via DoseEvent

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

from pbisim import ModelBuilder, PBIModel, solve_ode, DoseEvent, DoseSchedule
from pbisim.analysis.single import plot_pk

T_END = 48.0   # h

BASE_B = (
    ModelBuilder(n_bacteria=1, n_phages=0)   # bacteria only — isolate antibiotic effect
    .with_growth_rates(1.0, bacteria_to_resource_ratio=1e9)
    .with_nutrient(monod_constant=0.3, recycle_fraction=0.0)
)
B0 = np.array([1e8])   # dense inoculum so antibiotic effect is clearly visible

2 The with_antibiotic() API¶

with_antibiotic() registers one drug at a time. Multiple calls accumulate drugs into a list; build() assembles PKConfig and PDConfig automatically.

PK parameters (shared across strains):

  • k_elim (h⁻¹): first-order elimination from the central (blood) compartment
  • Vc (L): central compartment volume — converts drug amount to concentration
  • k12, k21 (h⁻¹): distribution to and from peripheral (tissue) compartment

PD parameters (per strain):

  • emax (h⁻¹): maximum bactericidal kill rate
  • ec50: drug concentration producing 50% of maximum effect (same units as Vc-derived concentration)
  • hill: Hill coefficient — steepness of the concentration–response curve

Biological meaning of the Hill equation: kill_rate(C) = emax · C^hill / (ec50^hill + C^hill)

  • emax is the "ceiling" kill rate — set to the maximum biologically achievable
  • ec50 ≈ MIC (minimum inhibitory concentration)
  • hill > 1: "all-or-nothing" kill once concentration crosses MIC; hill = 1: gradual, proportional response (most common)

# Ciprofloxacin-like parameters (concentration-dependent fluoroquinolone)
cfg_abx = (
    BASE_B
    .with_antibiotic(
        "ciprofloxacin",
        k_elim = 0.5,    # h⁻¹  — half-life ≈ 1.4 h (IV ciprofloxacin)
        Vc     = 15.0,   # L    — volume of distribution ≈ 15 L (IV)
        k12    = 0.4,    # h⁻¹  — distribution to peripheral tissues
        k21    = 0.1,    # h⁻¹  — redistribution back to blood
        emax   = 5.0,    # h⁻¹  — maximum kill rate
        ec50   = 0.5,    # mg/L — half-maximal effect concentration ≈ MIC
        hill   = 1.0,    # — linear concentration-response
    )
    .build()
)

# 400 mg IV dose (typical ciprofloxacin dose) → Ac_0 = 400 mg in Vc=15L → Cc_0 = 26.7 mg/L
dose_iv = DoseSchedule([DoseEvent(time=0, amount=400.0, target="antibiotic", index=0)])

cfg_abx_dosed = (
    BASE_B
    .with_antibiotic("ciprofloxacin",
                     k_elim=0.5, Vc=15.0, k12=0.4, k21=0.1,
                     emax=5.0, ec50=0.5, hill=1.0)
    .with_dose_schedule(dose_iv)
    .build()
)

res_abx = solve_ode(
    PBIModel(cfg_abx_dosed, initial_B=B0, initial_P=np.zeros(0), initial_S=1.0),
    t_end=T_END, dt=0.25,
)

fig, axes = plot_pk(res_abx)
fig.suptitle("Ciprofloxacin PK — central and peripheral compartment concentrations",
             y=1.02)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Cc_peak = res_abx.get("Ac0")[0] / 15.0
print(f"Peak central concentration: {Cc_peak:.1f} mg/L  (at {50*T_END:.0f}×ec50 = {50*0.5:.1f} mg/L)")
print(f"Kill rate at peak: emax × C/(ec50+C) = 5 × {Cc_peak:.1f}/({0.5+Cc_peak:.1f}) ≈ {5*Cc_peak/(0.5+Cc_peak):.2f} h⁻¹")

3 Bactericidal effect — comparing emax and dosing frequency¶

A concentration-dependent antibiotic like ciprofloxacin kills most effectively when peak concentrations are high (even briefly). Key insight: the ratio Cmax / MIC predicts outcome better than time above MIC.

Below we compare:

  • A single 400 mg dose (high peak, short duration)
  • 200 mg given twice (lower peak, more sustained)
  • No antibiotic (control)

Even though the total dose is the same, the single 400 mg dose achieves a higher peak concentration and faster initial killing.

In [3]:
def _run_abx(schedules_mg, label_list, t_end=T_END):
    results = []
    for events, label in zip(schedules_mg, label_list):
        dose_sched = DoseSchedule([
            DoseEvent(time=t, amount=mg, target="antibiotic", index=0)
            for t, mg in events
        ])
        cfg = (
            BASE_B
            .with_antibiotic("cipro", k_elim=0.5, Vc=15.0, k12=0.4, k21=0.1,
                             emax=5.0, ec50=0.5, hill=1.0)
            .with_dose_schedule(dose_sched)
            .build()
        )
        res = solve_ode(PBIModel(cfg, initial_B=B0,
                                 initial_P=np.zeros(0), initial_S=1.0),
                        t_end=t_end, dt=0.25)
        results.append((res, label))
    return results

regimens = [
    ([(0, 400)],               "400 mg ×1 (single dose)"),
    ([(0, 200), (12, 200)],    "200 mg ×2 every 12 h"),
    ([],                       "No antibiotic"),
]
results_abx = _run_abx(
    [r[0] for r in regimens],
    [r[1] for r in regimens],
)

fig, axes = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(12, 4))
for res, label in results_abx:
    axes[0].semilogy(res.time, np.maximum(res.get("B0"), 1), label=label)
    Cc = res.get("Ac0") / 15.0
    axes[1].plot(res.time, np.maximum(Cc, 0), label=label)

axes[0].set(xlabel="Time (h)", ylabel="Cells/mL", title="Bacteria")
axes[1].set(xlabel="Time (h)", ylabel="mg/L",
            title="Central antibiotic concentration Cc")
axes[1].axhline(0.5, ls=":", color="k", lw=0.8, label="EC50 = 0.5 mg/L")
for ax in axes: ax.legend(fontsize=9)
plt.suptitle("Ciprofloxacin dosing regimen comparison (B₀ = 10⁸, bactericidal)")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

4 Bacteriostatic effect¶

Some antibiotics (tetracyclines, macrolides, chloramphenicol) primarily inhibit growth rather than kill directly. In pbisim, bacteriostatic_emax multiplies the growth fraction frac:

growth_effective = μ · frac · (1 − bacteriostatic_emax · C^hill / (ec50^hill + C^hill))

At full bacteriostatic effect (bacteriostatic_emax = 1.0), growth is completely halted but bacteria remain viable. Combined bacteriostatic + bactericidal is common for drugs like doxycycline.

5 Per-strain resistance and dormant-cell activity¶

In multi-strain models, each strain can have its own emax and ec50. Resistant strains have a higher EC50 (shifted dose–response curve) or lower Emax (reduced maximum kill).

Dormant bacteria are notoriously tolerant to many antibiotics — they are metabolically inactive and lack the targets that antibiotics exploit (cell wall synthesis, ribosome activity, DNA gyrase).

emax_dormant and ec50_dormant set independent PD parameters for the dormant compartment (D). If not specified, dormant bacteria are unaffected.

cfg_resist = (
    ModelBuilder(n_bacteria=2, n_phages=0, n_depth=2)   # WT + resistant
    .with_growth_rates([1.0, 0.95], bacteria_to_resource_ratio=1e9)  # slight cost
    .with_dormancy(dormancy_rate=0.2, resuscitation_rate=0.1,
                   dormancy_signal="nutrient", resuscitation_signal="nutrient")
    .with_nutrient(monod_constant=0.3, recycle_fraction=0.0)
    # WT: sensitive; resistant mutant: 20× higher EC50
    .with_antibiotic("cipro", k_elim=0.5, Vc=15.0, k12=0.4, k21=0.1,
                     emax   = [5.0, 5.0],          # same Emax
                     ec50   = [0.5, 10.0],          # resistant: EC50 = 10 mg/L
                     hill   = 1.0,
                     emax_dormant = [1.0, 0.5],     # dormant somewhat tolerant
                     ec50_dormant = [2.0, 20.0])    # dormant: 4× higher EC50
    .with_dose_schedule(DoseSchedule([
        DoseEvent(time=0,  amount=400.0, target="antibiotic", index=0),
        DoseEvent(time=12, amount=400.0, target="antibiotic", index=0),
        DoseEvent(time=24, amount=400.0, target="antibiotic", index=0),
    ]))
    .build()
)

res_resist = solve_ode(
    PBIModel(cfg_resist, initial_B=np.array([1e8, 1e5]),
             initial_P=np.zeros(0), initial_S=1.0),
    t_end=48.0, dt=0.25,
)

fig, axes = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(12, 4))
LABELS = ["WT (EC50=0.5)", "Resistant (EC50=10)"]
for i, label in enumerate(LABELS):
    B_act = res_resist.get(f"B{i}")
    D_tot = sum(res_resist.get(f"D{d}_{i}") for d in range(2))
    axes[0].semilogy(res_resist.time, np.maximum(B_act, 1), label=f"{label} — active B")
    axes[1].semilogy(res_resist.time, np.maximum(D_tot, 1), label=f"{label} — dormant D")

for t_dose in [0, 12, 24]:
    axes[0].axvline(t_dose, color="k", ls=":", lw=0.8)
axes[0].set(xlabel="Time (h)", ylabel="Cells/mL", title="Active bacteria (dose markers ↑)")
axes[1].set(xlabel="Time (h)", ylabel="Cells/mL", title="Dormant bacteria")
for ax in axes: ax.legend(fontsize=8)
plt.suptitle("WT vs antibiotic-resistant strain; dormant tolerance (q12h dosing)")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

6 Summary¶

Parameter Role Typical values
k_elim Drug elimination from blood 0.3–1.0 h⁻¹
Vc Central compartment volume 5–30 L
k12, k21 Distribution to/from tissues 0.1–0.5 h⁻¹
emax Maximum bactericidal kill rate 3–10 h⁻¹
ec50 Half-max effective concentration ≈ MIC drug-specific
hill Steepness of kill curve (usually 1) 0.5–3
bacteriostatic_emax Max growth inhibition (0–1) 0.5–1.0
emax_dormant Kill rate for dormant compartment lower than emax
ec50_dormant Effective concentration for dormant kill higher than ec50

Clinical insight: The combination of phage (target: replicating bacteria, high burst-size amplification) with an antibiotic (target: dormant tolerant bacteria, residual resistant minority) is the theoretical basis of phage- antibiotic synergy (PAS) reported in clinical case reports.

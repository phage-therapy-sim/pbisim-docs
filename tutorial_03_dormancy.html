Tutorial 03 — Dormancy¶

Prerequisites: Tutorials 01–02

Many bacterial species survive antibiotic and phage treatment by entering a reversible non-replicating state called dormancy (also called persistence or quiescence depending on context). Dormant cells are metabolically inactive, divide slowly or not at all, and are less susceptible to phage infection.

pbisim models dormancy as a multi-depth stack of compartments D[0], D[1], …, D[Q-1], with bacteria diffusing inward (toward deeper dormancy) and outward (toward resuscitation) at a constant rate.

What you will learn

  1. The B ↔ D interface: entry and exit functions
  2. Built-in dormancy signals (nutrient, density, combined)
  3. Depth layers and diffusion (n_depth, dormancy_diffusion_rate)
  4. Attenuated phage adsorption in dormant bacteria
  5. plot_compartments for visualising the compartment breakdown

2 The B ↔ D interface¶

Bacteria transition between the active compartment B and the shallowest dormancy layer D[0] according to entry (dormancy) and exit (resuscitation) functions:

B  →  D[0]  at rate  dormancy_fn(S, B, config) × B
D[0] →  B   at rate  resuscitation_fn(S, B, config) × D[0]

These functions return a rate in h⁻¹; they can depend on nutrient concentration S, total active bacterial density, or neither.

The depth layers D[0], …, D[Q-1] exchange by symmetric diffusion (Fick's second law in discrete form) at a constant rate dormancy_diffusion_rate:

dD[d]/dt += diffusion_rate × (D[d-1] - D[d])   (inflow from shallower layer)
          + diffusion_rate × (D[d+1] - D[d])   (inflow from deeper layer)

The deepest layer D[Q-1] has no outward diffusion and is where natural death from dormancy (death_rate_D) is applied.

3 Built-in dormancy signals¶

Use the dormancy_signal and resuscitation_signal shortcuts in with_dormancy():

Signal name Entry rate ↑ when… Exit rate ↑ when…
'nutrient' (default) Nutrients scarce (S low) Nutrients plentiful (S high)
'density' Population dense Population sparse
'nutrient+density' Nutrients scarce and population dense Nutrients available and sparse
'constant' Always (constant rate) Always (constant rate)

Compatibility reminder: 'nutrient' and 'nutrient+density' require track_nutrients=True (Monod-family growth).

4 Depth layers and plot_compartments¶

Setting n_depth > 1 creates a stack of dormancy depth compartments. Bacteria diffuse inward (toward deeper dormancy) when stressed and outward (toward resuscitation) symmetrically. This models the empirical observation that prolonged dormancy increases tolerance: bacteria in D[Q-1] are the hardest to resuscitate and are where natural death from dormancy is applied.

plot_compartments shows the full B / D / I / H breakdown for one strain.

5 Attenuated phage adsorption in dormant bacteria¶

Dormant bacteria have reduced surface receptor expression, making them harder for phage to adsorb. Two parameters control this:

  • adsorption_rates_dormant (n_bacteria, n_phages) — baseline adsorption rate for dormant cells (typically 10–1000× lower than active).
  • attenuation_rate (n_bacteria, n_phages) — additional exponential decay with dormancy depth: rate_eff[d] = adsorption_dormant × exp(−attenuation × d).

Together they allow deeper dormant cells to be almost completely shielded from phage.

6 Custom dormancy and resuscitation functions¶

For models with more complex environmental sensing, you can supply any callable matching the protocol:

def my_dormancy_fn(S: float, B: np.ndarray, config) -> np.ndarray:
    """Returns per-strain dormancy entry rate (h⁻¹)."""
    ...
    return rates   # shape (n_bacteria,)

Pass it via dormancy_function= (or resuscitation_function=) in with_dormancy().

In [6]:
from pbisim import monod_growth

def threshold_dormancy(S, B, config):
    """Bacteria enter dormancy only when active density exceeds a threshold."""
    threshold = 5e7   # cells/mL
    B_total   = float(np.sum(B))
    rate      = 0.4 if B_total > threshold else 0.0
    return np.full(config.n_bacteria, rate)

# so it emits a UserWarning when track_nutrients=False is set alongside a
# custom function.  The warning is expected here — our function does not use S.

        ModelBuilder(n_bacteria=1, n_phages=1, n_latent=5, n_depth=2)
        .with_growth_rates(1.2, bacteria_to_resource_ratio=1e9)
        .with_phage_params(adsorption_rates=2e-9, burst_sizes=50.0,
                           latent_periods=0.5, phage_decay_rates=0.1,
                           adsorption_rates_dormant=1e-9)

                       resuscitation_rate=0.1,
                       resuscitation_signal="constant")

7 Oscillatory dynamics from deep-dormancy death and nutrient recycling¶

When death_rate_D > 0 (bacteria die in the deepest dormancy layer) and recycle_fraction is high, the following cycle emerges:

  1. Active bacteria enter dormancy and diffuse toward deeper layers.
  2. Bacteria in the deepest layer die, releasing nutrients back to the pool.
  3. The recovered nutrients allow surface-layer bacteria to resuscitate and grow.
  4. The new cohort of active bacteria re-enters dormancy — repeating the cycle.

This loop produces oscillations whose period is set by the diffusion time across the depth stack and the death rate in the deepest layer. The phenomenon explains oscillatory growth curves seen in stationary-phase cultures.

8 Pseudolysogeny¶

Pseudolysogeny is a quiescent phage-carrier state that some phages adopt when they infect nutrient-starved or metabolically inactive bacteria. Instead of immediately progressing through the latent chain, the phage genome persists without replicating — the infected cell becomes hibernating (H). When nutrients recover, the cell resumes lytic progression (H → I → lysis).

Pseudolysogeny is OFF by default — most obligately lytic phages show no such behaviour. Enable it with .with_pseudolysogeny(hibernation_rate, lytic_resumption_rate).

Note: the H→I rate is always ≥ resuscitation_rate regardless of pseudolysogeny settings. Dormant bacteria that get phage-infected (D→H, always open) must be able to wake up. lytic_resumption_rate is additive acceleration on top of that baseline.

Parameter Meaning Default
hibernation_rate Rate I→H (infected active → hibernating) [h⁻¹] 0 (off)
lytic_resumption_rate Extra H→I acceleration above resuscitation_rate [h⁻¹] 0

9 Summary¶

Builder call What it controls
.with_dormancy(dormancy_rate=..., dormancy_signal='nutrient') Entry rate at B→D[0] interface
.with_dormancy(resuscitation_rate=..., resuscitation_signal='density') Exit rate at D[0]→B
ModelBuilder(n_depth=Q) Number of depth layers
.with_dormancy(dormancy_diffusion_rate=...) Constant symmetric diffusion rate between layers
.with_phage_params(adsorption_rates_dormant=..., attenuation_rate=...) Phage shielding in dormancy
.with_dormancy(dormancy_function=fn) Fully custom entry function
.with_death(death_rate_D=...) + recycle_fraction Deep-dormancy death + recycling → oscillations
